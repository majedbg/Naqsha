# Naqsha — Design Context

> **Naqsheh (نقشه)** — the painted grid-sheet a Persian carpet designer hands to a weaver.
> A single sheet can be woven at different scales, in different palettes, by different
> workshops, and every carpet that comes off the loom belongs to the same family while
> carrying its own variations. **Naqsha is that same transaction, running through a pen
> plotter or laser cutter instead of a loom.**
>
> The designer works on paper. The machine works in ink or light. The pattern lives in
> the space between them.

This document is the source of truth for every design decision in this project. Before
writing any interface code, re-read this file. When a new component, color, type
choice, motion, or interaction is proposed, it must pass the principles below.

---

## Design Context

### Users

**Two related personas, one product:**

- **The playful beginner.** A hobbyist with an AxiDraw or a desktop laser, making things
  for the joy of making them. They pick a pattern, nudge parameters, watch the plotter
  draw, and feel delight. They want exploration without friction.
- **The intermediate craftsperson.** Honing a design they plan to run as a small
  batch — stickers, cards, wood-cut ornaments, small editions. They care about
  repeatability, preview accuracy, plot time, and overlap warnings. They want
  precision without sterility.

**Context of use.** Sitting at a desk, probably in a home studio, a plotter whirring
next to them or a laser humming behind glass. Unhurried. Attentive. Often iterating —
design, preview, plot, look, adjust. Not a dashboard user. Not an enterprise user.
Someone who chose a *slow* output device on purpose.

**Job to be done.** Turn an idea into a physical thing the plotter or laser can
faithfully reproduce — with enough control to trust the machine, and enough play to
discover something they didn't plan.

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** *playful, patient, reliable.*

**Voice.** Encouraging, unhurried, specific. Never jargon-heavy, never cute to the
point of irritating. Speaks to the maker like a knowledgeable bench partner, not a
cheerful assistant and not a terminal.

**Emotional goal.** *Patient delight.* Calm and grounded in motion and layout — nothing
snaps or flashes — but with moments of warmth and color that feel painted, not
rendered. Sitting down to Naqsha should feel like sitting down to graph paper and a
tin of gouache: quiet, then colorful.

### Aesthetic Direction

**The visual anchor is the naqsheh itself.** Ruled white paper. A faint grid. Broad
stretches of empty cream ground. Color placed as *painted cells* — saturated, rare,
meaningful — never as gradient washes or ambient glows. Pattern emerges from
arrangement, not ornament.

**References.**
- Persian carpet naqsheh cartoons (the images in the brief). Cream paper, visible
  graticule, hand-painted cells in madder red, cobalt, saffron, olive, rose, burgundy,
  bone.
- The physical object of graph paper with gouache.
- Old loom pattern books and illuminated manuscript grids.
- The composure of Muji packaging, but with more color and warmth.
- Risograph print — color as discrete, slightly-imperfect ink passes.

**Anti-references. This product must NOT look like:**
- Another Figma / Adobe / "creative tool" dark panel clone.
- Crypto / AI slop: dark backgrounds with cyan glow, purple→blue gradients, neon
  accents, "futuristic" chrome.
- A dashboard. No card grids of KPIs, no sparklines as decoration, no "hero metric"
  templates.
- A children's toy. Playful ≠ cartoonish. Warmth ≠ bubbly.

**Theme.**
- **Light mode is the default.** It matches the naqsheh anchor — paper is the ground.
- Follow system preference on first load.
- A visible theme toggle lives in the UI (to be added). Dark mode exists but is
  secondary: deep indigo / ink paper rather than pure black, with the same jewel-tone
  accents muted slightly.

**Color direction (placeholder teal is out).**
- Ground: warm off-white / cream, tinted very slightly toward the brand hue.
- Grid: a faint warm gray graticule, visible but never loud.
- Ink: a deep indigo-black for primary text and drawn strokes (evokes iron-gall ink,
  not `#000`).
- Accent palette: a small, opinionated set of naqsheh-derived jewel tones — madder
  red, cobalt blue, saffron gold, rose, olive green, burgundy. These are used
  *sparingly and discretely*, like painted cells, not as ambient tints.
- Interactive affordances (active slider thumb, checked checkbox, selected layer) use
  one load-bearing accent at a time to draw the eye.

**Typography direction.**
- Body: a warm, slightly humanist grotesque with optical sizing — readable at small
  sizes, with a hint of handmade quality. Avoid the Inter / DM / Plex / Outfit /
  Instrument reflex list. Candidates to audition in craft mode: **Bricolage
  Grotesque**, **Söhne**, **ABC Diatype**, **Darker Grotesque**.
- Display: something that evokes a hand-painted manuscript caption — quiet character,
  not ornamental clutter. Candidates to audition: **Redaction** (press-imperfect),
  **Bluu Next**, **Tanker**, **Bagnard**. Final pick gets made in craft mode against
  the real layout.
- No monospace as lazy "technical" shorthand. Numbers in the plot-metrics UI can use
  tabular lining figures from the body face.

### Design Principles

Every design decision must satisfy at least one; none may violate any.

1. **Paper first, panel second.** The ground is cream paper with a faint grid, not a
   flat tinted card. Layouts breathe on the page. Not everything needs a container,
   and containers are hairlines on paper, not drop-shadowed rectangles.

2. **Color as punctuation.** The naqsheh is ~80% white ground. Color appears like
   painted cells — rare, saturated, placed. No gradients for decoration. No glowing
   accents. A single accent at a time, load-bearing.

3. **Grid as substrate, not cage.** The interface honors a visible rhythm (a 4pt scale,
   aligned gutters, a subtle graticule where it serves the eye). But the grid is
   there to be *broken intentionally* — asymmetric compositions, generous negative
   space around things that matter.

4. **Patient motion.** Transitions decelerate like a hand setting something down.
   Ease-out-quart or quint, 240–360ms for medium moves. No bounce, no elastic, no
   snap. Reduced-motion respected.

5. **Precision you can see.** This is a craft tool for physical output. Text is
   high-contrast and legible. Interactive affordances (slider thumbs, checked boxes,
   selected layers, focus rings) are *obvious* — a painted cell you can see from
   across the room. Keyboard-first: every control has a visible focus state and a
   sensible shortcut.

6. **Human-in-the-loop, visible.** Every transformation shows preview → apply → revert.
   The UI never silently changes the user's work. State changes are announced
   calmly, with a way back.

7. **Named after what it does.** UX writing is specific and unhurried. "Estimate plot
   time" not "Calculate." "Overlaps found: 3" not "⚠ Warning." No emoji as decoration.
   No exclamation points. No "Oops!"

## Using this document

- **Craft mode** (`/impeccable craft …`) reads this file before designing anything.
- When auditioning fonts, palettes, or motion curves, check the result against the
  **Anti-references** and **Design Principles** before committing.
- This file is living. Update it when a deliberate direction shifts — not when a
  single component needs an exception.
