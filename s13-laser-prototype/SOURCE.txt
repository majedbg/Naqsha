Why it was a good candidate:
  - Real-world, openly-licensed (CC0), high-resolution photograph.
  - Shot roughly frontally, high contrast, unambiguously PERIODIC and GEOMETRIC
    (square lattice of stars/X-motifs with a coarser quatrefoil sub-lattice) —
    exactly the "Islamic geometric star-tile panel" profile the milestone asks
    for, and a clean input for lattice + symmetry detection.

Important limitation this photo exposed (see README.md, "Fidelity findings"):
  - It is LIGHT stone with DARK openings. Naqsha binarizes dark = ink and has no
    invert/polarity control, so the pipeline traced the DARK OPENINGS (and the
    connecting gap-web), i.e. the negative space, rather than the light stone
    members. The extraction is faithful to the dark ink, but a viewer expecting
    the stone members sees the "dual" pattern. This drove follow-up issue #2.
